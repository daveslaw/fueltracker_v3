# Cashier Guide

This guide is for cashiers at Elegant Amaglug, Speedway, and Truck Stop. As a cashier your job is to enter the POS Z-report totals, record dry stock sales and closing stock counts, and submit the cashier portion of the shift.

## Getting started

After logging in you are taken to the cashier view for the current shift. The screen shows the shift date, period (morning or evening), and a progress checklist with three items:

- **Fuel POS Z-report** — required before you can submit.
- **Dry stock POS Z-report** — optional, but recommended.
- **Stock count** — optional, but recommended.

The three sections are always accessible from the navigation list on this screen.

> **Note:** You only need to complete the **Fuel POS Z-report** to be able to submit. The other two sections are optional. However, completing all three gives the owner a complete picture of the shift.

---

## Step 1: Fuel POS Z-report

The fuel POS Z-report captures the sales totals from the fuel point-of-sale system for this shift period.

1. From the cashier hub, tap **Fuel POS Z-report**.
2. You will see a list of pumps at your station, each with two fields: **Litres sold** and **Revenue (ZAR)**.
3. Enter the litres sold and revenue for each pump as shown on your Z-report printout.
4. Optionally, take a photo of the Z-report using the photo button — this creates an audit record.
5. Click **Save** to save your entries.

After saving, you will be automatically redirected to the **Dry stock POS Z-report** step.

> **Tip:** Work through the pumps in numerical order and match each line on the printout to the corresponding pump number in the app.

> **Note:** Prices shown next to each pump are the current selling prices configured by the owner. These are for your reference only — enter the values from your printout exactly as they appear.

---

## Step 2: Dry stock POS Z-report (optional)

This step captures sales of dry stock products (e.g. drinks, snacks, oils) from the POS system.

1. From the cashier hub, tap **Dry stock POS Z-report**.
2. You will see a list of active products configured for your station, each with a stock code and description.
3. For each product, enter **Units sold** and **Revenue (ZAR)** from your printout.
4. Optionally, take a photo of the dry stock Z-report.
5. Click **Save**.

If you did not sell any dry stock products during this shift, you can skip this step and go directly to the stock count or submit.

---

## Step 3: Stock count (optional)

The stock count is a physical count of how many units of each dry stock product you have on hand at the end of the shift.

1. From the cashier hub, tap **Stock count**.
2. A list of active products is shown. Each row displays the product name, stock code, and the **opening count** (how many units were on hand at the start of the shift, from the prior shift's closing count or a baseline set by the owner).
3. For each product, enter the **closing count** — the number of units physically present at the end of the shift.
4. If a delivery of dry stock was received during this shift, click **Add delivery** next to the relevant product and enter the quantity received.
5. Click **Save count** when all products have been counted.

> **Tip:** Count every product on the list, even if you think the number hasn't changed. Accurate counts allow the system to calculate dry stock variance correctly.

---

## Submitting the shift

Once the Fuel POS Z-report is saved (and optionally the dry stock steps), you can submit.

1. Return to the cashier hub by tapping **Back** or navigating to the hub screen.
2. Check that **Fuel POS Z-report** shows **Complete** in the progress list.
3. Click **Submit shift**.

The button will be greyed out if the Fuel POS Z-report has not been saved. Once submitted, the screen shows the submission time and the **Submit shift** button is replaced with a confirmation message.

> **Warning:** Submitting is final. If you made an entry error, let your supervisor know. They can submit a correction from the shift summary page.

---

## Viewing your submission summary

After submitting you can view a read-only summary of what was recorded:

1. From the cashier hub, tap **Summary** (or navigate to the summary step in the progress bar).

The summary shows:

- **Fuel Sales** — POS litres sold and revenue per fuel grade.
- **Dry Stock** — for each product: opening count, deliveries, POS units sold, expected closing, actual closing, and the physical variance (units and ZAR value). Shown in green (no variance), amber (overage), or red (loss).

---

## Troubleshooting

**The Submit shift button is greyed out.**
The Fuel POS Z-report has not been saved yet. Go to **Fuel POS Z-report**, complete all entries, and click Save.

**I can't find my shift.**
Your shift is linked to your station. If no shift appears, the supervisor may not have created the shift yet, or you may be logged in with the wrong account. Contact your supervisor or owner.

**I entered the wrong litres or revenue on the Fuel POS Z-report.**
If you have not yet submitted, go back to **Fuel POS Z-report** and update the figures, then save again. If you have already submitted, inform your supervisor — they can correct the values from the shift summary.

**A product is missing from the stock count list.**
Only active products configured for your station appear in the list. Contact your owner if a product should be listed but is not.

**I received a dry stock delivery but forgot to record it during the stock count.**
If you have not yet submitted, go back to **Stock count**, find the product, and click **Add delivery**. If you have already submitted, let your owner know so they can account for it.
